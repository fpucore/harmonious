CMDC (Centrally Managed Digital Currency)
A digital currency and asset class,
built on Gobble blockchain technology.


CMDC is the acronym for Centrally Managed Digital Currency and is stylized as $CMDC.

The framework for CMDC was started in 2020. It is a prototype digital currency and asset class with a specification which pegs CMDC to a defined existing currency to determine its value. Due to the way it was developed, it was inherently flawed. The original value rate was proposed to be 1:1.25. For example, 1 US dollar would equate to 1.25 CMDC. However, big development changes to the mining process have now altered the original rate. The value rate is now 1:2500.

Bitcoin is infamous for being energy and resource intensive during the mining process. Some analysts believe that its high rate of energy use could see its eventual demise. CMDC has been designed specifically to be light on system resources for both mining and storage. This helps increase energy efficiency and ease the storage burden while still being able to mine large amounts. During the mining process it may push all cores of the central processing unit to maximum but memory usage remains moderate. While there is no technical limitation to the total number of CMDC blocks that can be put into circulation, there are still restrictions of the amount of available blocks in holding in the social distribution vaults. Currently, this is limited to 1 trillion CMDC per social distribution vault, across a maximum of 4 vaults.

CMDC is mined using a parallel processing and file duplication combination. Mined blocks are created from a raw block, called "block0". To prevent fraudulent duplication of blocks, each individual block is dated and timestamped which cannot be altered through traditional file duplication methods of the operating system. Once the mining process is complete, mined blocks are then refined. Without being refined, the blocks are worthless. During the refining process the blocks are compressed, encrypted and digitally signed with a unique and secret key which is not made public. It is this secret key which is used to identify authenticity of CMDC block sets.

Once blocks have been mined, refined, encrypted, digitally signed and verified, they are moved into a social distribution vault for circulation. We are currently testing software which enables buying of CMDC and verification of transactions. We are still developing software which enables selling and trading. CMDC operates on our private blockchain, called Gobble Chain. In March 2022 it received its first major upgrade to version 2.0.

April 2022, we introduced three new tokens - CICADA Community Token, POWER8 Token and TIDEIN Token. Token options are now fully integrated into CMDC and Gobble Chain. Token options are mined and refined through a similar process to regular CMDC block sets, but have multiple layers of encryption, use a different verification method to include a unique tokenID and are valued differently. Tokens are limited in supply.

A new area of development focus will include integrating support for non-fungible tokens[?], or NFTs. The specification for NFT integration with our blockchain is yet to be finalized and more information will be provided when available. We aim to offer NFT integration which will be applicable to original creations in the forms of digital images, photos, audio, video and other digital artwork creations, which can be sold as NFT by rights holders and bought using CMDC. The proof of rights and the transaction will be integrated into Gobble Chain.

FAQ about $CMDC
1. Is it a currency or an asset?
We treat $CMDC as an asset as it is just one component of the Centrally Managed Digital Currency platform.

2. If I buy, can I sell?
No. Currently we only offer options to buy $CMDC.

3. Will I lose money?
We treat $CMDC as an asset. We do not consider money invested through purchasing $CMDC to be a loss.

4. Where does the money go?
All funds from purchasing $CMDC are directed into a dedicated holding account.

5. Is it right for me?
Only you can decide! We develop the Centrally Managed Digital Currency platform to deliver a stable, secure, transparent platform for digital assets and currency. However, much like traditional stock and crypto markets, the potential for financial loss is real and liability for any losses is the responsibility of consumers.
