June 24, 2022

We have successfully upgraded Gobble Chain to version 3.0.
This was not anticipated and come as a result of some very unexpected circumstances.
Late yesterday the blockchain crashed because of technical reasons that were unforeseen and of which we never developed for.
We had recently introduced a new backup mechanism which duplicated the current running blockchain into an archive of its own.
Initially things looked promising but we seriously misjudged just how quickly the running blockchain grows and equally misjudged how quickly disk space would be consumed by duplicating it for archival, without any compression.
It was primarily the lack of disk storage space on the server which caused the entire blockchain to stop functioning and effectively break.
It was an oversight which we readily admit should not have happened and that is on us.
This morning we were forced to dump version 2.0 entirely and quickly adopt version 3.0, and make some necessary changes to see it implemented with confidence.
It has been a complete success.
Version 3.0 of Gobble Chain replicates the exact same behavior as its predecessor, but with some critical modifications.
The amount of disk space its predecessor consumed had become unmanageable for the resources that we have and to be honest, it was just unnecessarily bloated by design - something we never understood, until yesterday.
Version 3.0 introduces a much leaner design, functions much lighter by using a half-load data addition model to the blockchain while using less system resources, consumes so much less disk space and introduces a brand new compressed archival feature.
The new blockchain is now operational and running successfully.
The changes have no effect on current investments of CMDC and its values, which all remains unchanged by the implementation of this new version.
So, as we said, this update comes much earlier than we anticipated, but we were presented with an immediate challenge and are proud of what we have been able to do to meet that challenge.

June 14, 2022

Mozilla Firefox has been certified for use with Harmonious Platform Systems.
The two candidates for certification were Mozilla Firefox and Brave Browser.
This was a challenging decision for us, considering both candidates are respectable web browsers.
Mozilla Firefox has been certified over Brave Browser on the basis that we feel Mozilla, through Firefox and as an organization, is more dedicated to the freedom movement than what Brave Browser is.
This is no way negates any efforts that Brave Browser has made and continues to make, especially with its focus on security and privacy, but we feel their intentions are more focused towards improving their own products and services rather than the freedom movement as a whole.
We actually considered certifying both candidates, however, we definitely feel more work needs to be done with Brave Browser to reach the point where we are completely satisfied it can be certified for use with Harmonious Platform Systems.
Because we have not yet reached that point of satisfaction, Brave Browser will not be certified at this time.
We will continue to monitor the development progress of Brave Browser and if significant improvements are made we are open to certify its use in the future.

May 15, 2022

We are currently reviewing the "Harmonious Access Browser" component of the specification.
We had plans to ship a custom web browser with modifications made specifically to suit the Harmonious Platform.
However, after much consideration we have decided that shipping a custom browser is both unnecessary and negated by the fact that good web browsers already exist that already function well with Harmonious Platform.
In place of shipping a custom web browser, we'd like to endorse an existing web browser and certify it's compatibility with Harmonious Platform.
We have not yet finalized which web browser we will certify, but both Mozilla Firefox and Brave Browser remain the two most likely candidates.

February 10, 2022

We have completed our investigation into a recent security breach that we detected.

This investigation is now closed.
